# Fair Housing — Language Guardrails for Realty Stack Skills

Any skill that produces realtor copy — listings, follow-ups, blog posts, social, neighborhood briefs — must check the draft against this file before returning it.

This isn't legal advice. It's the practical compliance baseline every working agent already operates under. The skills enforce it so the realtor never has to remember every word in the moment.

---

## Federal protected classes (Fair Housing Act + amendments)

Discrimination is prohibited based on:

1. **Race**
2. **Color**
3. **National origin**
4. **Religion**
5. **Sex** — including gender identity and sexual orientation (HUD 2021 guidance + Bostock v. Clayton County 2020)
6. **Familial status** — presence of children under 18, pregnant individuals, anyone in the process of securing custody
7. **Disability** — physical or mental impairment that substantially limits one or more major life activities

Source: 42 U.S.C. § 3601 et seq. (Fair Housing Act of 1968 as amended).

---

## Michigan additional protected classes

Michigan's Elliott-Larsen Civil Rights Act adds:

- **Age**
- **Marital status**
- **Height and weight** (Michigan is one of very few states that protects these)

Some Michigan municipalities (e.g., Grand Rapids, Ann Arbor) add **source of income** and other classes. When in doubt, default to the stricter local rule.

## Phrases to NEVER use in marketing copy

The following are presumptively discriminatory under HUD's advertising guidance because they steer based on protected class:

| Problem phrase | Why it's a problem | Use instead |
|---|---|---|
| "Perfect for families" | Familial status steering | "3 bedrooms, fenced backyard" (describe the feature) |
| "Great for couples" / "starter home for newlyweds" | Familial / marital status steering | "Compact 2-bedroom layout" |
| "Bachelor pad" / "bachelorette" | Sex / marital steering | (Avoid entirely; describe the home) |
| "Empty nesters" / "perfect for retirees" | Age / familial steering | "Single-floor living, low maintenance" |
| "Active adult community" | Age steering (unless property qualifies for 55+ exemption) | Use only if 55+ HOPA exemption applies; cite the exemption |
| "Quiet neighborhood" | Frequently coded for familial status (no kids) | "Cul-de-sac street" or describe specific traffic conditions |
| "Safe neighborhood" | Race/national-origin coding risk; also legally unprovable | (Avoid; let the buyer evaluate) |
| "Walking distance" | Disability discrimination concern | "0.4 miles from..." (use measurable distance) |
| "Master bedroom" | Industry has largely moved away (race/slavery association) | "Primary bedroom" |
| "Exclusive community" / "exclusive neighborhood" | Race/national-origin coding risk | (Avoid; describe specific features instead) |
| "Traditional family neighborhood" | Familial status + potentially religious | Describe the neighborhood specifically |
| "Christian neighborhood" / any religious framing | Religious steering | (Never) |
| "English-speaking neighborhood" | National origin steering | (Never) |
| "No Section 8" / "no vouchers" | Source-of-income violation in many MI jurisdictions | Treat all qualified income equally |
| "Must be employed" (without further qualification) | Source-of-income concern | Specify the underwriting criteria neutrally |

## Phrases that are OK (feature-focused, not class-focused)

- Bedroom count, bathroom count, square footage
- Lot size, fence type, garage capacity
- School district (factual; not "you'd want this district if you have kids")
- Specific named amenities (granite counters, hardwood floors, etc.)
- Measured distances ("0.3 miles from downtown")
- Specific neighborhood facts ("on the Heritage Hill historic district")
- Property condition (move-in ready, needs cosmetic updates, fixer)
- Age of major systems (roof, HVAC, water heater)
- Tax info, HOA fees, utility costs

**Rule of thumb:** describe the property, not the imagined buyer.

## Steering rules

A realtor must not direct buyers toward or away from specific neighborhoods based on the protected class of the buyer or the existing residents.

**Don't do:**
- "This is a great neighborhood for a Black family."
- "You probably don't want to look in [X area]."
- "There's a Catholic church nearby that might be nice for you."

**Do:**
- Let the buyer specify their criteria. Show them everything that fits the criteria.
- Provide factual neighborhood information (school district ratings, commute times, walkability scores) without editorializing about who would "fit."

Skills that recommend neighborhoods to buyers must surface OBJECTIVE criteria the buyer named, not infer based on the buyer's apparent demographics.

## Advertising requirement

The Equal Housing Opportunity statement or logo should appear on listing-side marketing materials per HUD guidance. Skills producing listing copy or marketing assets should include the statement or remind the agent to add it.

**Standard text:** *"All real estate advertised herein is subject to the federal Fair Housing Act, which makes it illegal to advertise any preference, limitation, or discrimination based on race, color, religion, sex, handicap, familial status, or national origin, or intention to make any such preference, limitation, or discrimination."*

## Disability accommodation language

When discussing a property's accessibility, describe the features factually:

- "Step-free entry from garage" — good
- "Wheelchair-friendly" — risky, prefer "single-floor layout, 36-inch doorways"
- "Walk-in shower" — fine
- "Walking distance to..." — replace with measured distance

Never refuse to show a property based on a buyer's disability or assumed disability. Never ask whether a buyer has a disability.

## When a skill catches a problem

The skill should:
1. Surface the specific phrase or framing.
2. Explain briefly why it's a concern (one sentence).
3. Offer 1-2 alternative phrasings.
4. Let the realtor decide.

Skills do NOT silently rewrite — the realtor is the responsible party and needs to see the issue.

---

## When in doubt

The realtor is licensed and responsible. If a skill is uncertain whether a phrase crosses a line, surface the uncertainty rather than guessing. Default to caution; the cost of a flagged but legitimate phrase is a 30-second clarification, while the cost of a fair-housing complaint is far higher.

For ambiguous cases, suggest the realtor consult their broker or their state association (in Michigan: Michigan Realtors at mirealtors.com; NAR fair housing resources at nar.realtor/fair-housing).
